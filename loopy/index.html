Why I built this

I built this app for two reasons.

01

I'm a PM for a health tracking product — helping chronic patients monitor daily health metrics. Our biggest pain point: users don't log consistently and often drop off.

02

I wanted to track my own yearly goals. Tried habit trackers — quit after 2 weeks. Not because they were bad — I'm just lazy.

Then I realized: The problem isn't motivation — it's friction.

I became my own case study. Built something different.

Old Way

✕Open app → Wait for load → Find habit → Tick → Close. Too many steps.

✕Notification pops up → "Later" → Forget.

Loopy Way

✓Notification pops up → Tap "Done" right on the notification. No need to open the app.

Now I'm using it myself, observing my own behavior — testing if this approach actually reduces friction, so I can bring insights back to improve our product at work.
